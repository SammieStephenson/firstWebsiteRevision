Main Attraction's

Food for Everyone

Edmunds Oast

Charelston is known for it's amazing cusine. As recommened by locals Edmund Oast's Restaurant and Brewpub has an outdoor and indoor menu full of nationally recognized beer, handcrafted cocktails and upsacle food all with a perfect blend of historic charm and modern finishes. For Patrons 18 and up. Located in a warehouse district of North Charleston this evening will be on the pricier and formal side of your getaway. The original Edmund's Oast Brewing Company can give you all the luxury of the same great southern food and craft beer at a much more affordable rate; welcoming patron of all ages.



Couples Getaway

Sullivan's Island

Looking for more of a quieter beach? Sullivan's Island is a littel further out at about 30 minutes away from Charleston. This offers more seclusion to this intimate (2.5 mile long) beachfront. Sporting a quaint beach town with greenery lacing all beach access points for a truly romantic feel. Recommended for parties 18+

Boat Ride & Tour of Fort Sumter

Take a breezy romantic boat ride with your special someone across the Charleston Harbour out to the historcal National Park of Fort Sumter. Recognized for the first shots of the U.S.A's Civil War in the late 1800's. Most alluring at sunset. Recommended for parties of all ages.



Family Time

Folly Beach

Folly Beach is only a short 15-20 minute drive from downtown Charleston. Clink the link for multiple activities available for you, your kids or friends, Including fishing, kayaking, and even a day at the beachfront itself. Folly offers kid friendly restaurants and ice cream shoppes!

Charleston Museum & Heyward-Washington House

At the Charleston Museum you'll be able to explore the social, cultural, and natural history of Charleston and the surrounding area. You'll see that the City has taken extra precaution caring for some of it's ancient artifacts found locally. The Charleston Musuem also sponsors two houses studied and preserved from the 1700's, John Manigault House and Heyward-Washington House. Here you'll learn about how the upper-class Charlestonians lived and what their lives looked like almost two centuries ago, and also the history of the home itself. These homes feature intricate and romantic gardens to wander through. Recommended for all parties.
